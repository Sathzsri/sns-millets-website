The Superfood Profile of Kodo and Pearl Millets

March 02, 2026 • 6 Min Read • Health & Wellness

Nutritionists worldwide are consistently upgrading their dietary recommendations away from simple carbohydrates to highly specialized ancient grains. Let's dissect the nutritional powerhouse hiding inside Indian Kodo and Pearl Millets.

Kodo Millet (Paspalum scrobiculatum)

Known locally as 'Varagu' or 'Kodra', Kodo millet is uniquely high in dietary fiber—packing an astounding 9% per serving. It serves as a formidable antioxidant powerhouse.

  • Phytochemicals: Abundant in polyphenols which aid in actively suppressing inflammation.
  • Diabetic Control: Kodo has a naturally very low glycemic index (GI), making it an ideal primary staple for those seeking to mitigate blood sugar fluctuations.
  • Heart Health: High levels of magnesium help naturally relax blood vessels, reducing cardiovascular strain.
"A single serving of Kodo millet covers 15% of your daily iron needs, making it an extraordinary grain for combating widespread anemia across developing and developed nations alike."

Pearl Millet (Pennisetum glaucum)

Referred to commonly as 'Bajra' in Northern India, Pearl Millet is legendary for its raw energy output and thermogenic properties. It is immensely popular conceptually in winter menus because it actively keeps the core body temperature raised naturally.

  • Protein Champion: Pearl millet contains up to 14% protein per serving—one of the highest ratios among true grains.
  • Iron & Zinc: Exceptionally dense in trace minerals required for healthy immune functionality.
  • Gluten-Free: Safe for Celiac patients, allowing them to enjoy dense, bread-like baked goods without digestive trauma.

Why Source from India?

The soil profile across the Deccan Plateau naturally synthesizes these trace minerals into the millet crops dynamically. Without needing artificial soil injections, naturally occurring zinc, iron, and magnesium find their way into the harvest perfectly intact.
