Finger Millet (Ragi) – The Ancient Indian Supergrain Global Buyers Are Choosing in 2026

March 25, 2026 • 6 Min Read • SNS Global Traders Insights

Proprietor – SNS Global Traders

Ever since we started SNS Global Traders, there is one specific grain that international buyers ask me about more than any other. That grain is Finger Millet, which we call Ragi here in India. It really doesn't surprise me anymore because once you see the health benefits it offers, it makes complete sense why the demand is so high.

I want to give you a clear and honest picture of Finger Millet today. We'll cover what it is, why so many health brands are looking for it, how our local farmers grow it, and why it makes a smart sourcing choice for your retail or manufacturing business.

Understanding Finger Millet

Finger Millet (scientific name Eleusine coracana) is one of India's oldest cultivated grains. It has been grown across states like Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh for thousands of years. The name actually comes from the shape of the crop's ear head, which looks a bit like the fingers of a hand.

You might hear it called by different names depending on the region. In Kannada and Tamil it is Ragi, in Hindi it is Mandua, and in Telugu it is Ragulu. However, in the international export market, everyone simply knows it as Finger Millet.

Raw finger millet grains sorted and ready for export grading.

The Nutritional Value

The nutrition profile is where Ragi truly shines. Research consistently shows that Finger Millet holds its own against staple grains like wheat and rice, and in many categories, it actually beats them.

Here is what makes it so special:

  • Exceptional Calcium Content: Ragi is one of the richest plant-based sources of calcium among all cereals. This makes it incredibly popular for markets focused on bone health and dairy-free diets.
  • High Quality Protein: The grain contains crucial amino acids that your body needs to function properly.
  • Naturally Gluten-Free: It is an ideal ingredient for the fast-growing gluten-free food sector worldwide.
  • Rich in Dietary Fibre: It supports digestive health and works very well for low-GI diet formulations.
  • Infant Nutrition: In India, we have used malted Ragi for generations as a natural weaning food for babies. This traditional practice is now gaining serious traction globally.

How We Grow It In India

As a sourcing partner, I think it is very important for buyers to understand how their imported grain is produced. Transparency is key to building trust.

Finger Millet thrives with just average rainfall and is cultivated across multiple seasons. This makes it a very reliable, year-round supply crop for us. Our Indian farmers use a mix of traditional wisdom and modern crop management practices to ensure healthy yields. The crop is harvested carefully in stages so only mature, high-quality grain enters the supply chain.

After harvest, the Ragi is dried, machine-cleaned, and processed through sortex machines before we even think about exporting it. That is the exact standard we maintain at SNS Global Traders for every single shipment.

Global Market Demand

Through my interactions with buyers from Europe, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia, I have seen Finger Millet being sourced for a variety of interesting products.

  • Health food brands use it for gluten-free flour and puffs.
  • Baby food manufacturers rely on it for malted ragi formulations.
  • Organic food retailers stock it as a clean-label whole grain product.
  • Commercial bakeries use it for fortified cereals and snacks.
"The demand isn't just a temporary trend. We are seeing a sustained, fundamental shift toward ancient grains with proven nutritional value."
