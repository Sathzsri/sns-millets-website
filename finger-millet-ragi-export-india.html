What Is Finger Millet (Ragi)?

Finger Millet, scientifically known as Eleusine coracana, is one of India's most ancient cultivated grains — grown across Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and other states for thousands of years. Its name comes from the shape of its ear head, which resembles the fingers of a hand.

In India, it goes by many regional names: Ragi in Kannada and Tamil, Kelvaragu in some Tamil dialects, Mandua in Hindi, Nachni in Marathi, and Ragulu in Telugu. For international buyers, it is universally recognised as Finger Millet.

Why Is Finger Millet So Nutritionally Powerful?

This is where Ragi truly stands out. According to research from Tamil Nadu Agricultural University, Finger Millet is nutritionally comparable to — and in several ways superior to — wheat and rice.

Here is what makes it special:

  • 🌾 Exceptional Calcium Content — Ragi is one of the richest plant-based sources of calcium among all cereals and millets. This makes it highly relevant for markets focused on bone health and dairy-free diets.
  • 💪 High in Protein — The major proteins in Finger Millet are prolamins and glutenins, which contain all the essential amino acids your body needs.
  • 🌿 Naturally Gluten-Free — An ideal grain for the fast-growing gluten-free food segment worldwide.
  • 🫀 Rich in Dietary Fibre — Supports digestive health and is suitable for low-GI diet formulations.
  • 🧬 B-Vitamins and Minerals — A good source of essential micronutrients that are increasingly in demand in functional food markets.
  • 👶 Traditional Use in Infant Nutrition — In India, malted Ragi (Ragi Malt) has been used for generations as a weaning food for babies. This traditional practice is now gaining traction globally as a natural infant nutrition ingredient.

How Is Finger Millet Grown in India?

As a sourcing partner, I believe it's important for our buyers to understand how the grain they're importing is actually produced. Transparency builds trust.

Finger Millet is grown across two major conditions in India — irrigated and rainfed. It thrives with an average rainfall of 450–500 mm and is cultivated across multiple seasons, making it a reliable, year-round supply crop.

Indian farmers use both traditional and modern crop management practices — from seed treatment with bio-fertilizers like Azospirillum to structured irrigation schedules — to ensure healthy yields. The grain is harvested in two stages to ensure only mature, quality grain enters the supply chain.

Post-harvest, Ragi is dried to a moisture content of around 12% (or as per buyer requirements), machine-cleaned, and sortex processed before export. This is exactly the standard we follow at SNS Global Traders before every shipment goes out.

What Global Markets Are Buying Ragi For

In my interactions with buyers from the Middle East, Europe, Southeast Asia, and beyond, I have seen Finger Millet being sourced for:

  • Health food brands — for gluten-free flour, flakes, and puffs
  • Baby food manufacturers — for malted ragi formulations
  • Organic food retailers — for clean-label whole grain products
  • Food processing industries — for fortified cereals, snacks, and baked goods
  • Ayurvedic and natural wellness brands — for traditional health formulations
